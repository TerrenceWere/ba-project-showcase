## üéØ Analytical Framework

### Hypothesis Testing
1. **Nutrition ‚Üí Attendance**: Proper nutrition improves school attendance
2. **Attendance ‚Üí Performance**: Regular attendance correlates with academic success
3. **Program Design ‚Üí Impact**: Specific meal components drive different outcomes

### Statistical Models
- **Multiple Regression**: Isolating program impact from other factors
- **Time Series Analysis**: Tracking changes pre/post implementation
- **Cluster Analysis**: Identifying student segments with greatest need

## üí° Key Insights Delivered

### Quantitative Findings
- Strong correlation (r=0.72) between meal consistency and test scores
- 45% higher improvement for students from food-insecure households
- Optimal meal cost-effectiveness at $0.85 per student per day

### Qualitative Insights
- Teacher-reported improvement in student concentration and participation
- Parental support for program expansion and willingness to contribute
- Administrative challenges in program logistics and funding sustainability

### Policy Recommendations
1. **Program Expansion**: Scale to 15 additional schools based on need assessment
2. **Menu Optimization**: Adjust nutritional composition for maximum impact
3. **Community Engagement**: Parent contribution model for sustainability
4. **Monitoring Framework**: Ongoing data collection for continuous improvement
