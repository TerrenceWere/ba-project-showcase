# Project Charter: School Meals Impact Analysis

## Social Mission
To evaluate the effectiveness of school feeding programs in Nairobi and provide evidence-based recommendations for improving educational outcomes through better nutrition.

## Project Objectives
1. **Measure Impact**: Quantify the relationship between school meals and student performance
2. **Identify Best Practices**: Determine optimal program design and implementation
3. **Provide Recommendations**: Deliver actionable insights for program improvement and expansion
4. **Establish Framework**: Create sustainable monitoring and evaluation system

## Success Criteria
### Educational Outcomes
- 10%+ improvement in attendance rates
- 8%+ increase in academic performance
- 25%+ reduction in hunger-related absenteeism

### Program Effectiveness
- 90%+ of students receiving nutritionally adequate meals
- 80%+ stakeholder satisfaction with program
- Cost per student below $1.00 per day

## Stakeholder Ecosystem
### Primary Stakeholders
- **Students**: 2,000+ across 5 pilot schools
- **Parents & Guardians**: Decision-makers and program supporters
- **Teachers & School Staff**: Daily implementation and observation

### Secondary Stakeholders
- **Education Officials**: Policy makers and program funders
- **Community Leaders**: Local advocates and influencers
- **NGO Partners**: Implementation support and funding

## Scope & Boundaries
### In Scope
- Quantitative analysis of student performance data
- Qualitative research with stakeholders
- Nutritional assessment of meal programs
- Cost-effectiveness analysis
- Policy recommendation development

### Out of Scope
- Direct program implementation
- Fundraising activities
- Long-term program management
- Individual student interventions

## Ethical Considerations
### Data Privacy
- Anonymization of student personal information
- Secure data storage and handling protocols
- Informed consent for all participants

### Research Ethics
- Non-disruption of educational activities
- Cultural sensitivity in data collection
- Benefit to participating schools and students

## Resources & Constraints
### Available Resources
- Access to school records and performance data
- Cooperation from education department officials
- Volunteer research team and data analysts
- Existing program infrastructure and historical data

### Constraints
- Limited budget for additional data collection
- Time constraints aligned with academic calendar
- Sensitivity around student performance data
- Political considerations in public education

## Risks & Mitigation
| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Data quality issues | High | Medium | Data validation protocol, multiple sources |
| Stakeholder resistance | Medium | Low | Early engagement, clear benefits communication |
| Ethical concerns | High | Low | IRB review, privacy protocols, transparency |
| Resource constraints | Medium | High | Phased approach, volunteer coordination |
